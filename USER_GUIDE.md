## 1. Welcome to SAFE-8

### What is SAFE-8?

The **SAFE-8 AI Readiness Assessment Platform** is a comprehensive evaluation tool designed by Forvis Mazars to help organizations assess their preparedness for artificial intelligence adoption and implementation.

SAFE-8 evaluates your organization across **eight critical pillars** of AI readiness:

1. **STRAT - Strategy**: AI vision, goals, and strategic alignment
2. **DATA - Data Management**: Data quality, governance, and accessibility
3. **TECH - Technology Infrastructure**: Systems, tools, and scalability
4. **SKILL - Skills and Talent**: Team capabilities and training
5. **PROC - Processes**: Workflows, documentation, and optimization
6. **GOV - Governance**: Policies, compliance, and risk management
7. **CULT - Culture**: Innovation mindset and change readiness
8. **ETH - Ethics**: Responsible AI, fairness, and transparency

### Who Should Use SAFE-8?

- **C-suite Executives** evaluating AI investment opportunities
- **IT Leaders** planning digital transformation initiatives
- **Department Heads** preparing teams for AI adoption
- **Organizations** seeking to benchmark AI maturity against industry standards

### What You'll Get

âœ… **Personalized Assessment** - Tailored questions based on your organization's needs

## Appendix A: Glossary

**AI (Artificial Intelligence)**: Technology that enables machines to perform tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as learning, reasoning, and problem-solving.

**AI Readiness**: An organization's preparedness to successfully adopt, implement, and benefit from artificial intelligence technologies.

**Assessment Type**: The level of evaluation (Core, Advanced, or Frontier) selected based on organizational maturity.

**Benchmark**: A standard or point of reference (such as industry average) against which performance can be compared.

**Dashboard**: Your personal homepage within the platform showing assessment history and statistics.

**Gap Analysis**: Identification of differences between current performance and desired/best practice performance.

**Industry Average**: The mean score of organizations in your selected industry who have completed similar assessments.

**Likert Scale**: The 1-5 response scale used in assessments (Strongly Disagree to Strongly Agree).

**Live Score**: Real-time calculation of your AI readiness score that updates as you answer questions during an assessment.

**Pillar**: One of eight key dimensions of AI readiness (Strategy, Data, Technology, Skills, Processes, Governance, Culture, Ethics).

**Pillar Score**: Your performance score (0-100%) for a specific pillar.

**Radar Chart**: Visual representation showing your performance across all eight pillars compared to benchmarks.

**Overall Score**: Your comprehensive AI readiness score (0-100%) calculated from all pillar scores using weighted averages.

**PDF Report**: Professional document containing your complete assessment results, analysis, and recommendations.

**Weighted Average**: Calculation method where some pillars contribute more to the overall score based on their importance.

## Appendix B: Assessment Pillar Details

### STRAT - Strategy (15% weight)

**What It Measures:**
- Clear AI vision aligned with business objectives
- Executive sponsorship and commitment
- Defined success metrics and KPIs
- Business case development for AI initiatives
- Strategic roadmap for AI adoption

**Why It Matters:**
Strategy provides direction and alignment. Without clear strategy, AI initiatives often fail to deliver business value or gain executive support.

**Improvement Actions:**
- Develop AI vision statement
- Secure executive sponsorship
- Create AI steering committee
- Define measurable goals
- Build business cases for initiatives

---

### DATA - Data Management (15% weight)

**What It Measures:**
- Data quality and accessibility
- Data governance framework
- Master data management
- Data infrastructure and pipelines
- Data security and privacy controls

**Why It Matters:**
Data is the fuel for AI. Poor data quality leads to poor AI outcomes ("garbage in, garbage out").

**Improvement Actions:**
- Implement data quality initiatives
- Establish data governance
- Create data catalog
- Build data pipelines
- Improve data accessibility

---

### TECH - Technology Infrastructure (15% weight)

**What It Measures:**
- Cloud computing capabilities
- Scalable infrastructure
- Integration capabilities
- Security systems
- Technology stack readiness

**Why It Matters:**
Appropriate infrastructure enables AI development and deployment at scale.

**Improvement Actions:**
- Migrate to cloud platforms
- Upgrade computing resources
- Implement API management
- Enhance security infrastructure
- Modernize technology stack

---

**Why It Matters:**
Skilled people are essential to develop, deploy, and maintain AI solutions.

- Provide advanced training
- Create centers of excellence
- Establish mentorship programs

- Change management capabilities

Efficient processes enable rapid AI experimentation and deployment.

- Implement change management
- Streamline workflows
- Identify automation opportunities

Governance ensures responsible, compliant, and controlled AI usage.

Culture determines whether AI initiatives gain adoption and deliver sustained value.
